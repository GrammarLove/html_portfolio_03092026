Subject pronouns

These pronouns indicate who is performing the action of the verb. In Spanish, subject pronouns can often be omitted because the verb conjugation indicates the subject.

  • Yo (I)
  • Tú (You)
  • Él/Ella/Usted (He/she)
  • Nosotros/as (We)
  • Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes (They)
    • Yo voy a cine muy a menudo.

      I go to the movies very often.

    • ¿Tú vas a la oficina en autobús?

      You go to the office by bus?

    • Ella siempre está ocupada a las once.

      She is always busy at eleven.

    • Nosotros pagamos la cuenta.

      We pay the check.

    • Ellas también quieren mirar televisión.

      They also want to watch television.

Object pronouns

These pronouns replace the direct or indirect objects in a sentence. They include:

  • Direct object pronouns

    These replace the noun that directly receives the action of the verb. They typically precede the verb or attach to infinitives.

    • Me (Me)
    • Te (You-informal)
    • Lo/La (Him/it; her/it)
    • Nos (Us)
    • Los/Las (They)
      • No me gusta la comida frita.

        I don’t like fried food.

      • ¿Te gusta el vino blanco?

        Do you like white wine?

      • ¿Ese señor, lo conoces?

        That gentleman, do you know him?

      • ¿Lo compramos?

        Should we buy it?

      • Mi abuela nos quiere mucho.

        My grandmother loves us a lot.

      • Los postres, ¿ellos no los comen?

        The desserrs, they don't eat them?

  • Inirect object pronouns

    These indicate to whom or for whom the action is performed. They follow the same placement rules as direct object pronouns.

    • Me/mí (to/for me)
    • Te (to/for you-informal)
    • Le (to/for him/her/you)
    • Nos (to/for us)
    • Les (to/for them/you all-formal)
      • ¿Para mí? ¿Para me?

        For me?

      • Mis amigas inglesas no me escriben nunca.

        My English friends don’t ever write to me.

      • ¿Te la voy a prestar.

        I'm going to lend it to you.

      • A ella le compre un anillo, pero a él no.

        I bought a ring for her, but not for him.

      • Ellos nunca nos escuchan.

        They never listen to us.

      • Ellos son mis hijos y les hablo en alemán.

        They are my children and I speak to them in German.

Reflextive pronouns

Used when the subject and object of the verb are the same, indicating that the action is performed on oneself.

  • Me (Myself)
  • Te (By yourself - informal)
  • Ti (For yourself - informal)
  • Se (Himself/herself/yourself - formal)
  • Nos (Ourselves)
    • Al llegar a casa, yo me preparé un café.

      When I got home, I prepared myself a coffee.

    • ¿Qué quieres hacer por te mismo?

      What do you want to do by yourself?

    • ¿Qué quieres hacer por ti mismo?

      What do you want to do for yourself?

    • A ella se gusta mucho a ella misma.

      She likes herself a lot.

    • Nosotros nos lavamos bien.

      We wash ourselves well.

Possessive pronouns

These indicate ownership and replace nouns that show possession.

  • Mi (My)
  • Mío/Mía (Mine)
  • Tuyo/a (yours - informal)
  • Suyo/a (His/hers/yours - formal)
  • Nuestros/as (Our, ours)
    • Este es mi libro.

      This is my book.

    • Este libro es mío.

      This book is mine.

    • ¿Los zapatos no son tuyos?

      Aren't the shoes yours?

    • Señores, estos abrigos son suyos.

      Gentlemen, these coats are yours.

    • Aquel carro es nuestro.

      This car is ours.

Demonstrative pronouns

These point to specific things or people, indicating their distance from the speaker.

  • Este/Esta (This (masculine/feminine))
  • Ese/Esa (That (masculine/feminine))
  • Aquel/Aquella (That over there (masculine/feminine))
    • ¿Señor, este abrigo es suyo?

      Sir, is this coat yours?

    • A mi hermano no le interesa esa clase.

      My brother isn't interested in that class.

    • ¿Te gusta esta o aquella?

      Do you like this or that?

Interrogative pronouns

Used to ask questions.

  • Qué (What)
  • Quién (Who/whom)
  • Cuál (Which)
    • ¿Qué les gusta a ellos?

      What do they like?

    • ¿Quién quiere llamar a mi abuelo?

      Who wants to call my grandfather?

    • Lee el menú y ve cuál quieres.

      Read the menu and see which one you want.

Relative pronouns

These connect clauses or phrases, referring back to a noun mentioned earlier. The most common is que (that/which). Spanish relative pronouns include que (that/which/who), quien/quienes (who), el que/la que/los que/las que (the one that), el cual/la cual/los cuales/las cuales (which), and cuyo/cuya/cuyos/cuyas (whose).

Indefinite pronouns

These refer to non-specific people or things, such as alguien (someone) or algo (something).

  • Alguien (Someone)
  • Algo (Something)
    • ¿Alguien habla español acqi?

      Does anyone here speak Spanish?

    • Tengo ganas de comer algo.

      I feel like eating something
