For example, "I have finished my homework" indicates that the action of finishing homework occurred before the time of speaking. The perfect tense is used to express completed actions or states, and it can be contrasted with other tenses such as the imperfect, which refers to past actions or states that are not completed.

Informal Future

The informal future verb form, often referred to as the periphrastic future, is formed using the verb "To go" conjugated in the present tense. For example, "I will go." This form is commonly used in informal contexts to express future intentions or plans. Unlike the formal future tense, which typically uses "will," the informal future is more casual and often lacks the formal structure of auxiliary verbs.

Abrir To open

Indicative Present of Abrir

  • Yo abro (I open)
  • Tú abres (you open)
  • Él/Ella/Usted abre (he/she opens)
  • Nosotros/as abrimos (we open)
  • Vosotros/as abrís (you open)
  • Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes abren (they open)

Indicative Preterite (past tense) of Abrir

  • Yo abrí (I opened)
  • Tú abriste (you opened)
  • Él/Ella/Usted abrió (they opened)
  • Nosotros/as abrimos (we opened)
  • Vosotros/as abristeis (you opened)
  • Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes abrieron (they opened)

Indicative Imperfect (was/were) of Abrir

  • Yo abría (I was opening)
  • Tú abrías (you were opening)
  • Él/Ella/Usted abría (they were opening)
  • Nosotros/as abríamos (we were opening)
  • Vosotros/as abríais (you were opening)
  • Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes abrían (they were opening)

Indicative Conditional (would) of Abrir

  • Yo Abriría (I would open)

  • Él/Ella/Usted abriría (they would open)
